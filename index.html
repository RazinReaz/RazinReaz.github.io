About Me

Hi everyone! I am Razin and I teach people Computer Science at the same university I graduated from!
Yes, right now, I am an adjunct lecturer at the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, BUET. I completed my undergraduate degree in July 2024 with a CGPA of 3.93/4.00. Looks like all those sleepless nights paid off!

My research interests lies in Computer Graphics, Visualization, Computer Vision, and Physically Based Rendering. Ever since I started programming, I always get a kick out of turning every new thing I learn into code. It's always fun watching my own little simulations in my computer screen. LissaJous curves, fractals, Fourier series, you name it. Helps me learn better too, as I am a visual learner. You can take a look at some of my projects in the Projects tab.

I also have a keen interest in Machine Learning and Deep learning. The fact that simple weight matrices can do wonders in recognizing patterns and learning features is mind-blowing! When I first learned about GANs, I was blown away by how they fool the discriminator while it gets better at spotting what's real and what's fake. The intuition behind other models like VAEs, CNNs, LSTMs are also deeply fascinating! I am especiallly interested in explaining the inner workings of a machine learning model visually.


A short overview of my research experience: I have experience building a deep learning model to predict earthquake intensity with only 3 seconds of seismograms as my undergraduate thesis. I also have experience collecting physiological data from more than 40 rickshaw-pullers using more than 7 sensors to analyze the impacts of heat exposure on them. More about it in the Research tab.
